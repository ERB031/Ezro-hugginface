Our Story

Built by friends on a mission to make work feel human again

Ezro.AI started when Nathaniel Gaul and Ethan Beller compared notes about burnout creeping into the teams they led. They set out to build a companion that nudges healthier rhythms, celebrates progress, and keeps leaders honest about the real cost of hustle.

Read the latest Say hello

2018

The spark

Nathaniel and Ethan met while leading remote teams. Their shared frustration: tools tracked output but ignored wellbeing.

2021

Research-first prototyping

They interviewed over 150 people managers, gathering rituals that kept teams energized without policing calendars.

Today

Ezro.AI in the wild

Tens of thousands of employees use Ezro.AI nudges to protect focus time, book restorative breaks, and normalize asking for support.

People-first design

Every workflow starts with interviews, not dashboards. Nathaniel obsesses over inclusive rituals that work whether you are hybrid, remote, or on-site.

Wellbeing meets performance

Ethan codes the intelligence layer, balancing ethical AI with evidence-based habit loops so healthy teams can also hit aggressive targets.

Shared impact

We donate 1% of revenue to community mental health partners and publish transparency notes each quarter.

Why we built Ezro.AI

We believe every organization can be ambitious without burning out the humans who power it. Ezro.AI blends coaching, automation, and culture design so healthier practices become the default—not an afterthought.

Explore our features

Community advisory board

Team members from cooperative businesses, unions, and employee resource groups provide quarterly feedback so Ezro.AI stays pro-worker by design.

Privacy we can stand behind

Individual wellness signals stay personal by default. Leaders only see aggregated trends, never someone’s private reflections.

Meet Nathaniel & Ethan

Nathaniel Gaul leads product and partnerships. He previously scaled people programs at mission-driven startups and believes team rituals should be co-created, not dictated.

Ethan Beller heads engineering and research. He champions transparent AI that augments, never replaces, the human judgement of managers and teammates.

Together, they are building Ezro.AI as a collective effort with customers, practitioners, and workers who want better ways to thrive at work.
