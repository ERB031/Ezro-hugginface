Why meeting-free Fridays are only the starting point

Reducing meetings is great, but psychological safety, rotating caretaking duties, and protected recovery time are what actually keep teams thriving.

  • Schedule equity
  • Worker-led change

Meeting-free Fridays create breathing room, but they only work when paired with workload transparency and boundaries that people actually feel safe using.

Teams we partner with pair no-meeting days with shared planning rituals, explicit coverage plans for caregivers, and manager nudges to swap out marathon updates for async check-ins.

  • Rotate facilitation and note-taking to distribute invisible labor.
  • Celebrate when teammates use focus or recovery time to reinforce the norm.
  • Audit meeting invites quarterly and archive recurring sessions that no longer serve the team.
May 27, 2025 • Wellbeing

Designing benefits with whole-person health in mind

Stipends for therapy, subsidized caregiving, and sabbaticals are becoming table stakes. The real differentiator is how you help people actually use them.

  • Benefits access
  • Equity

Benefits matter when they are easy to find, understand, and activate. That means publishing plain-language guides, budgeting time for enrollment sessions, and checking in with employees who feel nervous about asking for help.

We encourage teams to track uptake by demographic so inequities surface quickly and to treat feedback from caregivers and chronically ill employees as required reading.

  • Bundle wellness stipends with suggested vendors that reflect diverse communities.
  • Offer confidential office hours so employees can ask questions without a crowd.
